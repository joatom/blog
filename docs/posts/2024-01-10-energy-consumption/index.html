Review of 2023

2023 is over, time to wrap up some numbers.

I’d like to share with you the numbers of my families energy consumption and \(CO_2\) footprint of 2023. For one reason, it is always interesting to glance at real personal data you usualy don’t get to see and I enjoy when I find others’ personal numbers on the internet and when I can compare myself to it. Secondly, publishing the data will put some pressure on me to further improve the numbers in 2024.

Our household consists of two adults and three kids.

Methodology and Data Sources

This section gives some additional information on how I come up with the data.

Energy consumption

  • Electricity: Data is taken from the electricity meters
  • Traval distance: Data is taken from car mileage display
  • Diesel consumption: Data is taken from car mileage display
  • \(CO_2\) emissions of Diesel in t: Calculation via “CO2-Rechner Des Umweltbundesamtes” (2024)

\(CO_2\) footprint

  • Emission is calculated via “CO2-Rechner Des Umweltbundesamtes” (2024).
  • Default values are taken for the categories General Consumption and Public Emissions.
  • Milage for Carpool and Public Transportation are approximated.
  • Categorie Food is calculated depending in age and diet.
  • Personal emission is either calculated by Houshold emission divided by five (Housing, General Consumption, Public Emissions) or by the actual values (Transportation, Food).
  • German average is taken from “CO2-Rechner Des Umweltbundesamtes” (2024).
